Understanding SWR, Bandwidth, and Q Factor

When using a Vector Network Analyzer (VNA) to characterize antennas (like a magnetic loop antenna), the following key parameters are critical:

1. Standing Wave Ratio (SWR)

The SWR measures how effectively power is being transferred to the antenna. An SWR of 1:1 indicates perfect matching, while a ratio of 2:1 is often considered acceptable. You can calculate SWR from the S11 parameter, which represents the reflection coefficient of the antenna.

2. Bandwidth

Bandwidth is defined as the range of frequencies over which the SWR is acceptable. Specifically, we calculate the bandwidth where the SWR remains less than or equal to 2 (SWR ≤ 2) or sometimes 3 (SWR ≤ 3), depending on the application's requirements.

How We Calculate These Parameters

Using the S11 data from the VNA:

  1. SWR is computed for each frequency using the formula above.
  2. The frequency range is then examined to find the points whereSWR ≤ 2 or 3.
  3. Bandwidth is calculated as the difference between the highest and lowest frequencies in this range.
  4. The Q Factor is determined based on the center frequency and the bandwidth.

These calculations help to assess the antenna's performance and identify the best frequencies for operation.
